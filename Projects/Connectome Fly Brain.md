## Goal

Biologically-inspired locomotion AI: a plastic neural controller that adapts in real-time to terrain changes, demonstrated on the NeuroMechFly model. Visualization in Unity shows anatomical fly with real joint data.

## Architecture

Two codebases in `C:/Users/neilt/Connectome Fly Brain/`:

### plastic-fly (Python)
- **FlyGym v1.2.1** runs MuJoCo physics simulation of Drosophila
- **CPG controller** (from `PreprogrammedSteps`) generates baseline gait
- **Sparse recurrent modulation net** (64-dim hidden, 80% sparse) adds plasticity on top
- **Hebbian learning** (lr=1e-5) lets plastic controller adapt to new terrain
- **Curator agent** filters significant results, tags Unity exports
- **Scientist agent** recommends next experiments (5-strategy deterministic)
- **Dashboard** generates 3-tab HTML (Monitor, Curator, Analysis)

### FlyBrainViz (Unity)
- Anatomical NeuroMechFly from MJCF XML + 71 STL meshes, built at runtime
- `FlyAnimator` plays back 42-DOF joint angles from experiment JSON
- `OrbitCamera` for interactive mouse camera (orbit/zoom/pan)
- `NeuralNetworkViz` shows sparse recurrent topology glowing with activity
- Side-by-side: plastic (adapting) vs fixed (frozen) controller

### Critical Finding: Gait Initialization
Brain-derived descending signals alone are **not sufficient** for locomotion. The gait pattern (tripod phase relationships `[0, pi, 0, pi, 0, pi]`) must be initialized at the motor/VNC level. Brain commands modulate speed/direction, but the coordinated leg pattern is a spinal-cord-level property. This mirrors real neuroscience: Drosophila locomotion is generated by VNC CPGs, the brain provides high-level modulation.

### Sensory Perturbation Results (9/10 tests pass)
Asymmetric sensory input activates the connectome's latent turn circuits. Key findings:

| Condition | Heading Change | Turn Drive Delta | Neural Asymmetry |
|---|---|---|---|
| baseline | +7.5 deg | -0.023 | -3.2 Hz (TL-TR) |
| contact_loss_left | +5.6 deg | **-0.078** | **-5.6 Hz** |
| contact_loss_right | +9.9 deg | -0.023 | -2.3 Hz |
| gustatory_left | **-2.2 deg** | -0.036 | -4.6 Hz |
| gustatory_right | +9.1 deg | -0.006 | -1.9 Hz |
| lateral_push | **+11.7 deg** | **-0.121** | -4.2 Hz |

- **Gustatory L/R contrast**: 11.3 deg heading separation -- brain distinguishes left vs right food signals
- **Vestibular strongest**: lateral push produces largest turn_drive shift (-0.121)
- **Contact loss creates neural asymmetry**: TL-TR rate shifts 3.2 Hz toward right-turning when left contact lost
- **Paper narrative**: "Connectome-derived descending pathway contains latent turn circuits that activate under asymmetric sensory conditions, consistent with stimulus-driven turning in real Drosophila"

### Olfactory Chemotaxis (v3 sensory: 275 neurons)
Expanded from 75 to 275 sensory inputs: +100 ORNs (50L/50R) + 100 visual R7/R8 (50L/50R) from FlyWire Codex annotations (Schlegel et al. 2024).

| Condition | Odor L-R | Lateral Displacement | Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Odor source LEFT | **+0.206** | **-0.696 mm (RIGHT)** | Avoidance |
| Odor source RIGHT | **-0.201** | **+0.470 mm (LEFT)** | Avoidance |

**Key finding**: Fly consistently turns AWAY from the odor source. Direction flips with stimulus side. This is **odor avoidance** behavior emerging from connectome wiring alone — no engineered navigation logic.

**Paper sentence**: "Odor signals injected into olfactory receptor neurons propagate through the connectome's antennal lobe and mushroom body circuits, producing turning behavior without any engineered navigation logic."

**FlyWire Codex data** (downloaded, 138,617 matched neurons):
- 10,853 visual sensory (R1-6, R7, R8)
- 77,521 optic lobe (L1, L2, Mi1, T4, T5, Tm...)
- 2,279 ORNs across 54 glomeruli
- 685 ALPNs + 429 ALLNs (antennal lobe)
- 5,177 Kenyon Cells (mushroom body)
- 334 gustatory, 2,659 mechanosensory

## Critical Lessons

- **Must use PreprogrammedSteps** for joint angles -- raw sine CPG causes physics instability (mjWARN_BADQACC)
- **Warmup needed**: ramp CPG magnitude from 0 over **3000+ steps** (500 only reaches 63% convergence with convergence_coef=20, dt=1e-4)
- **Tripod phase init**: `init_phases` MUST be `[0, pi, 0, pi, 0, pi]` -- zeros causes all 6 legs to synchronize (catastrophic gait failure, looks like bouncing)
- **Amplitude scaling**: `0.6 + 0.4 * forward_drive` (floor at 60%). Old `max(0.1, fwd)` was too aggressive
- **Brain window**: 20ms per brain step (was 10ms). Longer window gives more spikes for reliable readout
- **Coordinate conversion pitfall**: MuJoCo->Unity has det=-1 (improper rotation). Positions use M, but rotation axes/quaternion imaginaries are pseudovectors and need -M
- **Hebbian normalization**: divide outer product by hidden_dim, clamp pred_error to 1.0

## Brain-Body Bridge Architecture

```
Body obs -> SensoryEncoder -> BrainRunner (139k Brian2 LIF) -> DescendingDecoder -> CPG modulation -> Body
```

- **75 sensory neurons**: gustatory (20), proprioceptive (33), mechanosensory (15), vestibular (7)
- **47 readout neurons**: 22 annotated motor/descending + 25 connectivity-augmented supplements
- **5 decoder groups**: forward (10), turn_left (9), turn_right (9), rhythm (9), stance (10)
- **Config**: brain_dt=20ms, body_steps_per_brain=200, brain_warmup=200ms

## Sensory Hierarchy (upgrade path)

We currently have **Layer 1 only** (proprioception). The brain walks but doesn't navigate.

| Layer | Modality | Neurons | Status | What It Enables |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Proprioception | 75 | **Done** | Walking, perturbation response |
| 2 | Vision | ~9,800 (700 columns x 14 types) | Planned | Navigation, obstacle avoidance |
| 2 | Olfaction | ~50 receptor types | Planned | Chemotaxis, food seeking |
| 3 | Mechanosensory (detailed) | Johnston's organ, body hairs | Future | Wind response, flight |
| 3 | Nociception | Heat/pain neurons | Future | Escape behavior |

**NeuroMechFly v2** (Ramdya lab) already implements vision (FlyVision) and olfaction. Integrating their sensory stack into our encoder would unlock navigation behavior. This is the pitch for the Ramdya call.
